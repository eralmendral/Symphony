# Agile Delivery

## Mission

Convert ideas into shippable work without adding ceremony that slows a small team. Keep planning lightweight, visible, and tied to user outcomes.

## Workflow

1. Capture intake: problem, audience, desired outcome, constraints.
2. Split work into epics, user stories, tasks, bugs, or spikes.
3. Define acceptance criteria before implementation.
4. Estimate stories with relative story points.
5. Plan sprint scope from priority, capacity, risk, and dependencies.
6. Review delivered work against acceptance criteria and update the backlog.

## Story Point Guide

Use relative sizing:

- 1: trivial, known, low risk.
- 2: small, clear, limited files.
- 3: moderate change with a few unknowns.
- 5: larger feature, integration, or design work.
- 8: high uncertainty or broad change; split if possible.
- 13: too large for a healthy sprint story; split before commitment.

Story points are not hours. They combine effort, complexity, and uncertainty.

## Definitions

Ready:

- Problem and user value are clear.
- Acceptance criteria are testable.
- Dependencies and open questions are identified.
- Story is small enough to complete in one sprint.

Done:

- Code and docs are updated.
- Acceptance criteria pass.
- Tests or manual QA are complete.
- Review feedback is resolved.
- Release or rollout notes are captured when needed.

## Rules

- Do not over-plan unknown work; create a spike with a concrete output.
- Do not put implementation-only chores ahead of visible product risk without a reason.
- Prefer smaller stories over optimistic large estimates.
