# Agile Delivery

Symphony uses lightweight agile practices suitable for a solo founder or small engineering team.

## Cadence

- Backlog refinement: once per week or before sprint planning.
- Sprint planning: start of sprint.
- Daily check-in: short written or live update.
- Review: when work is ready to demo.
- Retrospective: end of sprint or after meaningful release.

## Roles

- Product owner: prioritizes outcomes and acceptance criteria.
- Engineer: implements, tests, reviews, and documents changes.
- Reviewer: checks correctness, quality, security, and maintainability.
- Release owner: verifies release readiness and rollback path.

For a solo project, one person may hold every role, but the checklist still applies.

## Issue Types

- Epic: outcome-sized body of work.
- Story: user-visible value.
- Task: implementation or operational work.
- Bug: broken expected behavior.
- Spike: time-boxed research.

## Story Points

Use points for relative effort, complexity, and uncertainty:

| Points | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Tiny, known, low risk |
| 2 | Small and clear |
| 3 | Moderate work with limited unknowns |
| 5 | Larger change or integration work |
| 8 | High uncertainty or broad impact; consider splitting |
| 13 | Too large; split before sprint commitment |

Do not convert points directly to hours. Use team velocity only after several completed sprints create evidence.

## Definition Of Ready

A ticket is ready when:

- The problem and expected outcome are clear.
- Acceptance criteria are testable.
- Dependencies and blockers are known.
- The work is small enough for one sprint.
- The affected platform or component is identified.

## Definition Of Done

A ticket is done when:

- Acceptance criteria pass.
- Code is reviewed.
- Tests or manual verification are complete.
- Documentation is updated when needed.
- Release notes or rollout notes are captured when needed.
- Jira and GitHub status match reality.
